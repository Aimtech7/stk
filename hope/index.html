Our Mission

To foster participation of indigenious communities to achieve social economic, cultural, political and enviromental justice.

Our Vision

We envision a future where collective rights of communities are recognized and their territories secured, and communities in the Mt. Elgon ecosystem are empowered to manage and govern their resources sustainably. 

OUR WORK

STK (Certified Public Benefit Organization - PBO) (STK) is a grassroots indigenous organization that works with and on behalf of communities within the greater Mt. Elgon ecosystem to demand collective social, economic, cultural, environmental, and territorial justice. Our Vision: Secure territories, empowered communities and sustainable resource governance within the greater Mt. Elgon ecosystem.

Protecting Women & Girls Rights

In promoting equity and recognizing the central role o of women as  agents of positive change across political and socio-economic spaces, we work to amplify women and girls' voices in critical social-economic decisions at the sub-national (county), national levels, and cross-border (regional).

Advancing Climate & Energy Justice

Mt. Elgon is a cool, high-rainfall mountain ecosystem and an important transboundary water catchment (Kenya and Uganda). Climate variability and change are increasing the risk of floods, landslides, and dry spells, disrupting farming and livestock keeping and putting pressure on household food and water security.

We support community-led climate resilience, ecosystem restoration, and equitable resource governance to protect livelihoods and reduce vulnerability.

Learn More

Enhance protection of Mt. Elgon ecosystem

The Mt. Elgon ecosystem is a vital montane forest and water-tower landscape that supports rivers, springs, and biodiversity within Mt. Elgon National Park and surrounding community lands. Communities depend on healthy forests, soils, and water for farming and livestock keeping.

Learn More

Defending Land, Environment and Territorial Rights

We work to defend and promote environmental and territorial rights linked to land access. Indigenous people and local communities seek to have their territorial and other rights respected, protected, and guaranteed. Our defense of territorial rights includes our ancestral lands

Learn More

Advancing Social, Cultural and Economic Rights

The constitution of Kenya under Article 174 (d) states that communities have the right to manage their affairs and to further their development.

Community organizing and empowerment is critical to enhance community participation in budget and policy processes.

Learn More

Cross-border Co-operation

Cross-border mobility is a resilience practice of pastoralist communities . Some of the efforts include;

  • Participatory mapping of local and cross-border transhumance corridors.
  • Strengthen cross border Advocacy
  • Support cross border communities dialogues and engagement.
  • Enhance women participation in cross border processes
